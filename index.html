Hierarchical MPC demo for Loadwise interview

Economics upstairs. Safety downstairs.

This browser demo shows how a two-layer controller could coordinate a data center with PV, BESS, flexible compute, and cooling assets against volatile German power prices and thermal limits. The data are synthetic, but the structure, constraints, and timing are chosen to be interview-grade and easy to explain live.

24 h / 15 min Upper layer economic horizon
60 min / 5 min Lower layer tracking horizon
Assets PV, BESS, flexible compute, cooling plant
Hard guardrails ASHRAE envelope, SOC reserve, rollback mode

Baseline scenario

Sunny day with an evening price spike

The controller charges the battery around the solar trough, pre-cools before the heat peak, and discharges into the evening scarcity window.

  • Upper layer target: move cost and carbon away from the evening ramp.
  • Lower layer target: hold inlet temperature within the recommended envelope while tracking the grid setpoint.
  • Demo point: show how forecast error triggers fast actuator correction without re-solving the full day plan.

What Makes This Production-Minded

Suggested Talk Track

What to say in 90 seconds

  • "I separated the problem because economics and actuator safety live on different timescales."
  • "The upper MPC reasons over 24 hours with market, weather, PV, and workload forecasts."
  • "The lower controller does not try to beat the market. It protects the plant while tracking the strategic plan."
  • "The hard constraints are thermal envelope, battery reserve, ramp limits, and rollback to a rule-based safe mode."
  • "In production I would swap these browser heuristics for a proper optimization service, but the interfaces would stay almost the same."

Demo Assumptions

The signals here are synthetic, but the framing is grounded in real operating assumptions: quarter-hour market resolution, volatile German wholesale prices, and a thermal envelope consistent with common data-center guidance. This is the right level for an interview demo because it shows architectural judgment, control intuition, and production thinking without pretending to be a certified controller.
