Interactive HMPC learning demo

Upper layer plans. Lower layer protects.

A modular browser demo for a data center with PV, battery storage, flexible compute, UPS losses, economizer support, and chiller-driven cooling. Each control below changes an explicit term in the power or thermal balance rather than just changing the chart styling.

Plant Controls

PV peak
Raises the daytime renewable supply available to the scheduler.
BESS energy
Changes how much energy can be shifted into the evening peak.
BESS power
Limits both strategic dispatch and lower-layer tracking trim.
Flexible load
Defines the fraction of IT load that can move across the day.
UPS efficiency
UPS losses are modeled as IT * (1 / efficiency - 1).
Fan intensity
Design fan power as a percent of IT load before cubic fan scaling.
Chiller COP
Mechanical cooling electric power is thermal load divided by COP.
Economizer
Higher values let cool ambient air offset more chiller thermal load.
Nominal inlet target
Lower targets protect thermal margin but increase cooling power.
Pre-cooling bias
Shifts the inlet target downward during cheap or cool hours.
Thermal mass
Higher values slow temperature drift and make pre-cooling more durable.
Price spread
Expands the day-ahead spread that drives charging and discharge incentives.
Disturbance
Injects a one-hour PV miss, heat spike, and load jump into the lower layer.
Zoom hour
Selects the one-hour lower-layer window shown in the tracking charts.
